World Overview

Increasing global temperature

Published at: 24 September

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in temperatures and weather patterns. Such shifts can be natural, due to changes in the sun activity or large volcanic eruptions. But since the 1800s, human activities have been the main driver of climate change, primarily due to the burning of fossil fuels like coal, oil and gas. Burning fossil fuels generates greenhouse gas emissions that act like a blanket wrapped around the Earth, trapping the heat and raising temperatures. The main greenhouse gases that are causing climate change include carbon dioxide and methane. These come from using gasoline for driving a car or coal for heating a building, for example. Clearing land and cutting down forests can also release carbon dioxide. Agriculture, oil and gas operations are major sources of methane emissions. Energy, industry, transport, buildings, agriculture and land use are among the main sectors causing greenhouse gases.

Carbon Footprint

Ride Bicycle - Reduce Carbon Footprint

Published at: 24 September

Cycling stands as an emblem of zero-emission transportation, making it a cornerstone for reducing air pollution and greenhouse gas emissions. Unlike cars that rely on fossil fuels and emit harmful gases, bicycles obviously have no emissions. This quality not only keeps the air clean and free from pollutants but also significantly reduces the carbon footprint of travel. One person alone will have little impact, but collectively pivoting to walking and cycling can have a great impact over time.

Systems in turmoil

Published at: 24 September

The turning point came in June 2024, when Bangladesh High Court re-instated a quota reserving 30% of government jobs for relatives of veterans from Bangladeshs 1971 war of independence. With government jobs in high demand due to elevated graduate unemployment, students protested the quota, which they also viewed as favouring supporters of Hasinas Awami League party. The Supreme Court eventually reduced the quota in late July to 5%.
